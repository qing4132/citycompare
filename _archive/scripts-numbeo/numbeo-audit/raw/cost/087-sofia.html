Do not be misled by the promoted "10% flat tax" narrative, because Bulgaria has formally moved into a phase of strict fiscal consolidation. The state now views well paid freelancers and business owners as a convenient revenue source to patch enormous budget gaps. For top earners, mandatory monthly social insurance payments currently sit near €660, but legislation already on the table will push this figure to approximately €783 later in 2026, significantly increasing the burden. This is a 20% increase within just a few months. This marks only the opening stage of a broader plan that also involves raising the dividend levy from 5% to 10% in order to finance a rapidly expanding deficit.

The overall budgetary environment is expected to deteriorate further as Bulgaria bows to pressure from the OECD and the IMF to fully abandon its flat tax framework. Instead of preserving simplicity, policymakers are clearly steering the system toward progressive taxation, a shift that disproportionately targets higher income individuals. These increases are intended to bankroll irresponsible public outlays, as defense spending alone is forecast to surge from roughly $1 billion in 2020 to an eye watering $7 billion by 2036. In practical terms, the government is funding its operations with money it lacks, and the shortfall will ultimately be transferred to you.

If you belong to the group of high income professionals, you will gain very little in exchange for these so called "Western" level payments. Infrastructure continues to deteriorate and the healthcare system is repeatedly ranked near the bottom within the EU. Relocating to a more stable EU jurisdiction where taxes translate into tangible public services is a far more rational choice. In Bulgaria, your payments primarily serve to offset the expenses of a state that has clearly lost fiscal discipline.
