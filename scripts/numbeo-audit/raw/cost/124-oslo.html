Living in Oslo – My Experience

The food culture in Oslo is quite disappointing. Prices are high, portions are small, and the overall value for money is poor. A simple meal for two at an average restaurant — without drinks, starters, or dessert — costs around 800 NOK.

Typical prices:

Caffè latte: 65 NOK

0.5L beer: 100–130 NOK

Glass of wine: 110–180 NOK

A gym membership usually costs between 500–900 NOK per month.

Transportation:

Public transportation in Oslo is decent but limited in options. They only offer a single ticket, valid for one hour, which costs 44 NOK. There are no cheaper short-trip options or flexible ticket types, which can make daily commuting expensive.

Housing:

I’ve been renting in Oslo for a few years, and the quality of rental properties is generally poor compared to the price. Many houses and apartments are badly insulated, which is surprising given the long, cold winters.

Most homes rely on electric heaters, which significantly increase expenses during winter, from around 3,500 NOK/month for a small place to +8,000 NOK/month for a larger apartment. Always look for accommodation with central heating, as it’s much more efficient and cost-effective.

Services:

Services are mediocre and expensive. For example:

Women’s haircut: 1,100–1,500 NOK

Men’s haircut: 900–1,300 NOK
